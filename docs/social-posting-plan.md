# Found in Alabama — Social Posting Plan

*Research-based plan for automated social publishing. Drafted June 2026 from 2024–2026 studies (Buffer, Sprout Social, Hootsuite, Metricool, Tailwind, platform-official statements) plus vintage-reseller practitioner sources. Confidence levels noted; weak evidence flagged.*

---

## 1. Research summary

### Posting frequency (small account, deep inventory)

| Channel | Recommended | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram feed | 3–5 posts/week | High (Buffer 2M-post study) |
| Instagram stories | 1–2 frames/day, most days; never 10+ at once | Medium |
| Facebook page | 3–5 posts/week | Medium |
| X | 1–3 posts/day (floor 3–5/week) | Medium |
| Pinterest | 1–5 fresh pins/day defensible for deep-inventory shops; official floor is 1/week | High floor / Low ceiling |
| BlueSky | 1–2 posts/day; replies matter as much as posts | Low (thin data) |

Key caveat: Metricool's 2026 study found reach falling ~31% industry-wide as posting volume rises — more posts ≠ more reach. Quality and sustainability beat volume (Mosseri: "twice a week for two years beats every day for two months"). No platform documents a spam penalty for frequency; the real costs are self-competition and audience fatigue.

### Timing (US Central, shopping/collectibles audience)

| Channel | Best windows | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Weekdays 12–3 pm (retail-specific: lunch-break shopping); Tue/Wed strongest | |
| Facebook | Weekdays ~9 am–2 pm; Tue/Wed strongest | FB is #1 product-discovery network |
| X | Tue–Thu 12–6 pm | Weekends weakest |
| Pinterest | Tue–Thu 10 am–1 pm | Timing nearly irrelevant — pins are search-driven and surface for months |
| BlueSky | Tue/Wed 9–11 am; ~6 pm secondary | Chronological feed → timing matters MORE here |

Skip Sundays everywhere. Magnitude check: Hootsuite's controlled experiment found ~30% more impressions from good timing — real but secondary to content. After a month of data, account-level analytics should override these defaults.

### Content differentiation

| Channel | Caption | Hashtags | Image | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IG feed | Hook in first 125 chars, story below | **3–5 max** (Dec 2025 hard cap of 5); niche tags like #vintagepyrex | 4:5 portrait; subject centered (3:4 grid crop) | Curated "treasure reveal" |
| IG story | Minimal overlay text | none | 9:16 | Casual, urgent — hauls, first dibs, countdowns |
| Facebook | ≤80–250 chars | **0–1** | 1:1 or 4:5 | Warm, nostalgic, plain facts: price/condition |
| X | 71–100 chars | **0–2** | 16:9 or 1:1 | Conversational — questions, hot takes on finds |
| Pinterest | Keyword-rich title (~40% of ranking weight) + 200-char description, keywords up front | **0** (keywords replace them) | **2:3 vertical (1000×1500)**, styled lifestyle shots | Evergreen/aspirational ("How to style brass candlesticks") |
| BlueSky | ≤300 chars | **2–3** (feed custom feeds — genuinely useful here) | flexible | Human, anti-corporate, soft-sell |

### Links back to product pages

| Channel | Strategy | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| IG feed | No clickable caption links → link-in-bio (rotate to current drop) | Confirmed |
| IG story | **Link sticker → product page** (available to all accounts; the one clickable organic link on IG) | Confirmed (IG official) |
| Facebook | Links in caption are downranked (Meta's own dashboard warns; 97% of top posts have no link). **Put link in first comment** | Confirmed |
| X | Historic link penalty confirmed by Musk (Nov 2024); reversed per X product head Oct 2025. Direct link OK now; strong hook text first | Confirmed but in flux |
| Pinterest | **Links are the point** — every pin links straight to the exact product page; outbound clicks are an official metric | Confirmed |
| BlueSky | No link penalty, officially stated ("post all the links you want"); 3–4x click engagement vs X reported | Confirmed |

### Content reuse / recycling

- Same item, same platform: wait **~21–30 days** before re-posting; a **re-styled new photo counts as fresh content** and resets the clock. (Scheduler-tool consensus, not platform-official.)
- Instagram/Facebook 2025–26 originality rules punish accounts whose output is mostly duplicated content — aimed at aggregators, but the safe move is reshoot/restyle, never re-upload the identical image.
- Stories are the cheap re-promotion channel: 24-hour lifespan means near-zero fatigue cost for re-promoting unsold inventory.
- Pinterest is the **best recycling channel**: "fresh pin" = new image + same URL. Re-pin unsold items with a new graphic indefinitely; pin to the most relevant board first, wait 2–7 days before other boards.
- Cross-posting the same item to all channels is fine; **stagger 24–72 h across platforms** rather than simultaneous blast.

### Vintage-niche tactics (practitioner evidence, directional)

1. Storytelling beats catalog posts — the "find" narrative: sourcing story → reveal → details → price/claim.
2. Behind-the-scenes content (estate-sale hauls, cleaning/before-after) builds anticipation pre-listing. Our haul journal posts map perfectly to this.
3. Education posts (how to spot real ironstone) break the sell-sell-sell pattern — aim ~1 in 4 posts being non-listing content.
4. Scheduled "drops"/collection launches with several days of story teasing outperform a constant trickle.
5. Niche hashtag communities: #vintageforsale, #thriftfinds, #resellercommunity, #shopsmall.
6. Natural light and real-life styling beat sterile product shots for this audience.

## 2. The proposed weekly plan

Steady-state target (~14 outbound posts/week + stories — all generated by the existing pipeline, so the cost is review, not writing):

| Day (CT) | IG feed | IG story | Facebook | X | Pinterest | BlueSky |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | — | 1–2 frames | 9–11 am | 12–2 pm | 1 pin | 9–11 am |
| Tue | 12–3 pm | 1–2 frames | — | 12–2 pm | 1 pin | 9–11 am |
| Wed | 12–3 pm | 1–2 frames | 9–11 am | 12–2 pm | 1 pin | 9–11 am |
| Thu | — | 1–2 frames | 9–11 am | 12–2 pm | 1 pin | 9–11 am |
| Fri | 12–3 pm | 1–2 frames | — | 12–2 pm | 1 pin | 9–11 am |
| Sat | 12–3 pm | 1–2 frames | 10 am–12 pm | — | 1 pin | — |
| Sun | — | — | — | — | — | — |

Weekly totals: IG feed 4, FB 4, X 5, Pinterest 6, BlueSky 5, stories ~10 frames.

**Content mix rule:** roughly 3 listing posts : 1 non-listing post (haul narrative, education, behind-the-scenes). Haul journal posts already auto-publish — promoting each new haul post is the natural non-listing slot.

**Item rotation rules:**
- A new item gets: 1 Pinterest pin (immediately — evergreen), then staggered over 1–3 days: IG feed or story, FB, X, BlueSky. Not all channels need every item.
- Unsold after 30 days: eligible for re-promotion — story frame (cheap), new-photo pin, or restyled feed post. Same-platform identical re-post never sooner than 21 days.
- Sold items: optional "SOLD — congrats" story frame (social proof, niche norm).

**Link rules (per channel, enforced by the pipeline):**
- Pinterest, BlueSky, X: direct product link in the post.
- Facebook: link moved to first comment (needs adapter support) or omitted from caption.
- IG feed: no link; caption says "link in bio" — keep bio link pointed at the newest drop or /products.
- IG story: link sticker to the product page (needs adapter support via Publer).

## 3. Automation design (to build)

Phased, building on the existing social_drafts queue:

1. **Phase A — Scheduler config + auto-scheduling.** A `posting_schedule` config (per channel: days, time window CT, weekly cap). New drafts get auto-assigned a `scheduledFor` slot in the next open window instead of waiting for manual "Post now." Vercel cron (e.g. every 15 min) publishes due drafts via the existing adapters.
2. **Phase B — Auto-draft generation triggers.** When the Chrome extension captures new items (or a haul post publishes), automatically generate channel drafts (existing Claude generator) and enqueue them under the rotation/stagger rules. Queue stays reviewable — drafts can require approval (toggle: full-auto vs. review-first).
3. **Phase C — Recycling engine.** Nightly job finds unsold items >30 days, not promoted in >21 days, and enqueues re-promotion drafts (story frames, fresh pins) with a per-item promotion history log.
4. **Phase D — Link-handling upgrades.** Facebook first-comment links and IG story link stickers via Publer API, if supported; bio-link rotation reminder.
5. **Phase E — Feedback loop (later).** Pull per-post engagement (Publer analytics API / platform APIs) to tune windows and frequency after 4–6 weeks of data.
