The sign out front said "Someone's Treasures — Estate Sale Coming Soon," and we got there on the first day, a few hours after opening. The sale was in Alexander City, and it belonged to a pilot.

The aviation theme ran through everything. There are two framed aircraft photos — one shows a propeller plane with what appears to be a signed inscription, the other a jet on a tarmac. There's a boxed Wings of Texaco 1929 Curtiss Robin Airplane, described as 6th in the series. A small red model airplane sits nearby, along with a photo album open to what look like airshow or flight-related snapshots. A couple of old football tickets — one marked Mississippi State, one Alabama — were in the mix too, along with a Holy Bible, a concordance, and some blue binders from what appears to be the Association of the Miraculous Medal.

Not pictured but worth knowing: we also brought home a big haul of LP records — local gospel, Elvis, and more.

Items will be listed across our shops as we get them cleaned up and photographed. Keep an eye on our storefronts and we'll have them up soon.
