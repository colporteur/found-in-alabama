Last week we packed out the estate of a retired Anniston physician. Three
generations of carefully kept things — every drawer organized, every book
in its place. The medical library alone filled two trucks: tooled-leather
case studies, anatomy plates, a near-complete run of mid-century JAMA.

The furniture leans Heywood-Wakefield era — walnut and atomic-print
upholstery, the kind of mid-century pieces that move fast on Etsy when the
photos are right. We lucked into a pair of armchairs in original fabric
that we're listing across three platforms.

And the ephemera — letters, prints, a few signed pieces — is the real
surprise. Including a framed Audubon print that the family had no idea was
the real thing.

pieces from this haul come online.
