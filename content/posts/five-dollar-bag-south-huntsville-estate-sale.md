The source photo tells the story pretty well — a brick ranch in Huntsville's Camelot neighborhood with a Loose Ends estate sale sign out front. We went on the final day for half-price deals, then circled back a few hours later when they offered a fill-a-bag special for five dollars.

The $5 bag ended up holding a crocheted piece, what looks like a small painted print in a plastic sleeve, a Casebus-branded case, some small figurines, and a handful of loose smalls that are still getting sorted out. Beyond the bag, most of what came home was framed artwork and prints — though two of those were promptly claimed by a teenager. The standout piece from the full haul is a vintage Huntsville Cotton Row Run poster co-branded with WZYP radio.

The owners appeared to have an interest in artwork and running, which tracked with what was on the tables. Keep an eye on our shops as things get cleaned up and listed — the poster especially is one worth watching for.
